2.11 - What ends a share dealing round?

Each player consecutively not making a purchase. The priority then goes to the player after the one who last made a purchase. In 1853 and 1853V2 he may sell the elephant that confers priority to another player.

5.1 - Where can you make an initial tile lay?

As for 1830.

5.2 - Can you lay two tiles in a turn?

First four companies only, and possible company 5 if it forms as a major company during the contract bidding stage. Each lay must be connected to a different station marker and the connecting routes must not use the same piece of track at any point. When they first operate, each of these companies must choose to either delay the ability to lay two tiles until Phase 3, or to gain the ability immediately. If the ability is gained immediately, the company must pay the bank every time that two tiles are laid. Company 1 must pay £50 to lay two tiles, companies 2 to 5 must pay £20 to lay two tiles.

5.3 - Must a tile replacement extend existing track?

Permissive; but upgrades only allowed on the company's 'patch'. A brown tile next to a grey tile may not be upgraded (i.e. there can never be two grey tiles adjacent to each other).

5.4 - Do villages upgrade?

Single villages do, sometimes becoming cities; double villages don't.

6.1 - Cost of station markers

0 (for both home hexes, in some companies), 40, 100.

6.2 - Can you lay more than one station marker per turn?

Not stated.

6.3 - Where can you lay a station marker?

The newly-laid station marker must be reachable from one of the laying company's existing station markers by an arbitrarily large train, and must be within the company's 'patch'. After the first '5' train is bought, companies owning at least one '5' or '6' train may place a station marker at any station that they can reach from a home base using one of their trains. A company placing a station marker outside its 'patch' must include that station in one of its routes in the same operating round.

6.4 - When is a company's first station marker laid?

When it first operates.

7.1 - Can you run into a city completely filled by rival station markers?

Yes.

7.2 - Can you do a run that passes through a city completely filled by rival station markers?

No.

7.3 - Unusual rules about running

Visit and get paid for villages without restriction.

7.4 - Can one train run to two stations on the same tile?

You may not run to two large stations on the same tile. Small stations are not restricted.

7.5 - Is double-heading allowed?

No.

7.6 - Rules about villages

Villages contribute to the run, but do not count towards the station count.

7.7 - Must the maximum possible revenue be claimed?

No.

8.1 - Does stock move right for payment of dividends?

One place if the total dividend is less than twice the share price; two to five places if the dividend is equal to or greater than two to five times the share price.

8.2 - What dividend payments go into the company's treasury?

None.

8.3 - Does stock move left for withheld earnings?

Yes, if shares of the company are in the bank pool.

8.4 - Can a company make a partial payout?

No.

9.1 - Can companies buy trains from one another?

Yes, minimum price 10, but '1M' trains may not be purchased by major companies.

9.2 - Must a major share company buy a train if it does not have one?

No; but its Director may choose to make up the balance from his own money if the company has insufficient credit for a train, leaving the company with an empty treasury.

9.3 - Can trains be sold back to the bank?

No.

9.4 - When a company is forced to buy a train and cannot buy one with its own means, what train may it then buy?

A company does not have to own a train.

9.5 - Must a minor company buy a train if it does not have one?

No.

9.6 - Can trains of the final type be purchased as soon as one train of the next-to-last type is purchased?

Yes.

9.7 - Can a company buy more than one train from the bank per OR?

Yes.

11.1 - Can you sell the Director's certificate into the bank pool?

Yes, and is then operated by official receiver if no other player becomes the Director.

13.1 - Game ends immediately with a bankruptcy?

No. A company does not have to own a train, so the player is never at risk of bankruptcy.

13.2 - What happens if the bank runs out of money during an operating round?

Game ends when the current OR is completed.

13.3 - What happens when stock first hits the top end of the market?

Game ends immediately.

13.4 - What happens if the bank runs out of money during a stock round?

Complete one more operating round.
